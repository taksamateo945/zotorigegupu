20 Best Nigerian Yoruba Movies You Must See in 2023

Publish date: 2024-02-14

The Nigerian film industry makes films in various Nigerian languages, of which Yoruba is one of them. These movies reflect the cultural and linguistic diversity of the country. The production of Yoruba movies in Nigeria dates back to early 1977 when Ola Balogun produced Ajani Ogun. Since then, some of the best Nigerian Yoruba movies have grown extensively and have crossed the shores of many countries.

Nigerian Yoruba movies have been modified into different genres like comedy, fiction, romance, action, etc. Viewers are guaranteed to get whatever they are looking for in a Yoruba movie. You need not worry about understanding the language, as most Yoruba movies have English subtitles. Here, we will be listing the best Nigerian Yoruba movies that you must see.

The movie has gotten both positive and negative reviews from critics. However, reports show that the movie performed well at the box office. Ayinla also received the 2021 African Movie Academy Awards for his achievement in cinematography.

    • What is the history of Yoruba movies?

    The history of Yoruba films dates back to the 1970s and the first Yoruba film was Ajani Ogun, made by Ola Balogun in 1977. Ija Ominira and Ajani Ogun are the two pioneering Yoruba feature-length films, and Aiye, a celluloid film was released in 1979 and produced by the late Chief Hubert Adedeji Ogunde. He was an actor, playwright, theatre manager, and founder of the first contemporary professional theatrical company in Nigeria, the African Music Research Party.

    Over the years, the Yoruba film industry, genially referred to as “Yorrywood”, has produced a diverse range of films that entertain and educate audiences of fluent speakers and non-speakers alike. These films are known for their cultural richness, exciting storylines, and almost accurate depictions of the Nigerian experience. The first Yoruba movies also include Jaiyesimi, Aropin N’tenia, and Ayanmo, feature-length films influenced by Yoruba mysticism.

     Is Yoruba Movies Under Nollywood?

    Yes, Yoruba movies are a part of Nollywood as Nollywood is a body that encompasses the totality of activities taking place in the Nigerian film industry. Yoruba movies have been an integral part of Nigeria’s movie industry for decades, from the days of travelling troupes and playing companies.

    Nollywood also includes films of the Igbo, Hausa, Edo, Efik, Ijaw, Urhobo, Ibibio, Annang or any other of the over 300 Nigerian languages”. Under Nollywood are also the Nigerian/African diaspora films considered affiliated with Nigeria or made specifically to capture the Nigerian audience.
